Game Day Parking

Parking can be one of the most stressful parts of game day, but planning ahead makes a huge difference. Here are the options I would recommend if you're visiting Lubbock for a Texas Tech football game.

My Favorite Option

Honestly, my favorite way to park is by using a website called Prked. You can rent a driveway from someone who lives close to the stadium, for much less than official parking lots. Most of the driveways are very close to the stadium, and you avoid sitting in most of the heavy traffic close to university lots.

Official Parking

If you'd rather park on campus, there are several official parking lots. Most of the lots closest to the stadium are reserved for season ticket holders, but some lots like the USA Auto Lot and the S-1 Lot sell single-game parking passes ahead of time. Just don't expect to show up on game day and find a spot in every lot because many of them sell out.

Free Parking

If you're trying to save some money, you can park for free in the lots near the United Supermarkets Arena or the Health Sciences Center. From there you can catch the Citibus shuttle to Raider Alley. The shuttle starts running about three hours before kickoff and costs around $6 through the GoPass app. Just know the shuttle lines can get pretty long before and after the game.

One More Tip

Another good option is parking near First Baptist Church on Broadway. The Walk is a little longer but you get to walk through campus on your way to the game, which is part of the experience on Gameday.

Game Day Tips

  • Arrive early to avoid traffic and parking delays.
  • Check the weather before leaving for the stadium.
  • Review the stadium bag policy before attending.
  • Wear Texas Tech colors and support the Red Raiders.
